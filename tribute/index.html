Dr.Norman Ernest Borlaug

(The man who saved a billion lives)

Here is the timeline of Dr.Borlaug's life:

  • 1914: Birth
    • March 25, 1914: Norman Ernest Borlaug was born in Cresco, Iowa, USA.
  • 1930s: Education
    • 1933: Enrolled at the University of Minnesota, initially studying forestry.
    • 1937: Earned his Bachelor of Science degree in forestry.
    • 1939: Received his Master of Science degree in plant pathology.
    • 1942: Completed his Ph.D. in plant pathology and genetics from the University of Minnesota.
  • 1944: Early Career
    • 1944: Joined the Rockefeller Foundation’s Cooperative Mexican Agricultural Program, focusing on improving wheat production in Mexico.
  • 1940s-1950s: Development of High-Yield Wheat
    • 1940s-1950s: Developed semi-dwarf, high-yield, and disease-resistant wheat varieties in Mexico, leading to a significant increase in wheat production.
  • 1960s: The Green Revolution
    • 1960s: Borlaug’s wheat varieties were introduced to India and Pakistan, greatly increasing food production and helping to avert famine in these countries, marking the start of the Green Revolution.
  • 1970: Nobel Peace Prize
    • 1970: Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to world food security and for his role in the Green Revolution.
  • 1980s: Advocacy and New Initiatives
    • 1984: Became a distinguished professor of international agriculture at Texas A&M University.
    • 1986: Co-founded the World Food Prize to honor significant achievements in improving food security.
  • 1990s-2000s: Continued Influence
    • 1990s: Continued advocating for agricultural innovation, particularly in Africa, where he aimed to replicate the successes of the Green Revolution.
    • 2000s: Remained active in global food security efforts, working with organizations and governments to promote sustainable agricultural practices.
  • 2005: Final Years
    • 2005: Continued his work and advocacy for global food security, even in his 90s, maintaining his commitment to fighting hunger.
  • 2009: Death
    • September 12, 2009: Norman Borlaug passed away in Dallas, Texas, at the age of 95.
